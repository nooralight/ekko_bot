Creating a philosophy and stance : An example from Team Coaching.

Created on 2023-08-30 12:03

Published on 2023-08-30 14:11

I recently completed a 9 month training program at Team Coaching Studio to further develop my competences as a Team Coach. I've been working on Team development in the corporate environment since 2017 and my work has mostly been 'facilitation' of team workshops. From the program I was seeking some guidance and practice to shift towards 'team coaching', as a complimentary skill set to 'facilitation'. To be clear; when 'working to grow / develop teams', sometimes 'coaching' is beneficial and sometimes 'facilitation' is more appropriate, choosing which depends on the situation and needs of the team.

'Team coaching' is a fascinating and still a budding profession and I chose this program as the 'competence content' is aligned with the ICF team coaching competences, that were published for the first time in 2022.

Here are some of the 'Team Coaching' competences that we learnt:

Ethical Practice & Contracting

Effective Relating / Psychological Safety & Trust

Direct Communication / Powerful Questions / Active Listening

Relationship Systems / Wider Context & Stakeholders / Power & Authority

Creating Awareness / Generating Results

See image below for the TCS Team Coaching Competence Wheel around which you can read these competences.

All of the above competences are so very valuable for a Team Coach to bring to the team with whom he / she is working, but as I progressed through the program I came to realize that the center of the Wheel was really the most valuable / crucial component to 'Being a Team Coach'?

At the center of the wheel is a Team Coaches 'philosophy and stance'. The philosophy is basically the 'beliefs / principles that guide us' as a team coach. The stance is 'what do we stand for?'

Throughout the program we crafted and sensed and re-crafted our personal philosophy and stance... it was quite a struggle (I never had a 'philosophy' before'..) But in the end it was so worth it and I have leaned on both of these during each and every team session that I have been in since.

Here is my philosophy :

I became a team coach because I believe in the ‘’power of teams’’… the power of a team to create impact.  I have worked with individuals and I have worked across whole organizations.  I believe that teams have the power to create impact for the individuals, with other teams, in the organization and more broadly in the system. 

For example, on a sports field teams can create impact.  Even if they don’t ‘win’, their impact can be felt.  See the above image of the Dutch 1974 world cup soccer team. They actually came 2nd, but they created ‘the beautiful game’ which was a style of ‘playing’ that is still influencing the way soccer is played nearly 50 years later.

I became a team coach because I believe in ‘’togetherness’’.  Throughout my life I have been attracted to the camaraderie of being with other humans and collaborating together to achieve something that we are passionate about.

I became a team coach because I believe in ‘’learning’’.  I am from a family absolutely full of teachers,,, I never wanted to be a teacher but ‘learning’ is in my blood.  I believe that teams are a perfect arena in which to learn… where team members can both leverage and apply their strengths and also dive into their learning edges in a ‘safe’ environment.  Teams are also  the perfect arena in which to experiment.

I became a team coach because I believe in ‘’Team creativity’’.  For me, creativity is joy!  Creativity is the art of enabling something new / original and valuable to emerge.  When teams give themselves the permission and space / time to dive into creativity, then magic can happen.  Without creativity there is no innovation…. If I can help teams to be creative and embrace possibilities, I feel alive.

And here is my stance:

  • Meet the team where they are.

  • Trust the WE and empower the team.

  • Be calm, this creates space for the team.

  • Slow down and focus on the figure that emerges.

  • Listen and act with kindness.

  • Focus on the positive and on the team strengths.

  • Have the knowledge available / hold frameworks and models lightly in my pocket.

  • Challenge the team to grow and become more effective.

  • Get the team out of their ‘heads’ … Embrace possibilities.

A Broader Reflection on Philosophy and Stance.

I've written this article about my philosophy and stance as a 'Team Coach', it confirms what I believe in and what I stand for as a 'Team Coach',,, it gives me guidance and something to lean on as I work, it's relevant in all aspect of my role. I can imagine it's also relevant for current and future clients to understand who I am / who they are working with.

More broadly, I can imagine that any professional, in any role,,, with any wheel of competences, could potentially have a philosophy and stance at the center of their wheel....! Why not?

An engineer, a lawyer, a team leader, a CEO, a sales person etc... You probably already have a list (or wheel) of competences,,,, If you don't have a philosophy and stance, at the middle of your wheel, to guide you and lean on.... Why not start crafting one?
